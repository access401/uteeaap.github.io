Timeliness

Here's some relevant information from OCR Complaint No. 04-17-2381 (.pdf) for considertation. (See item 4)

In its Corrective Action Plan, the University will acknowledge that if all inaccessible content and functionality identified during the Audit is not removed or made accessible on a timely basis, the University will be in violation of this Agreement, Section 504, and Title II and OCR may initiate administrative enforcement or judicial proceedings to enforce the specific terms and obligations of the Agreement

A "General" Solution To the Problem

In order to be able to get material to students in a timely manner, take advantage of any technology that will enable us to use off-the-shelf skill sets.

An example: Braille "translators"

The ability to deliver content in Braille is especially important because it serves as a stepping stone between high school and college. The problem is, not many people know Braille -- much less the fact that there are different "kinds" of Braille, some more appropriate than others for certain disciplines. The solution is to take advantage of technology that can do "translation" work for you.
