Visual Cues?

We're not generally consious of how much the eye and the ear work together to learn. In the example above, at the 0:28 time frame, the teacher makes a reference to a visual aid. In this case, he explicity referenced an image, but there are quite a few other times in which a visual reference would help in understanding the content. For example, at the time frame, 2:15, he shows a correspondence between the coordinates and the formula. It's easy to grasp when you see the visual correspondence, harder when you have to abstract out the concept.

How effective is this kind of teaching? A comment about this lesson follows, from the comments section

Example One

This is a great teacher doing great things with a great video. An assumption is that transcripts are a way for a blind person to be able to get the same content as a student with sight. But can he? Notice at the time frame of 1:50, the instructor says, "Let's go over here".... If you were blind and just listening to this, "how" would you know where "here" is?
