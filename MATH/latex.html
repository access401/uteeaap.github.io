LaTeX

Documentation of our examination of the use of LaTeX as a potential methodology to allow blind students to pursue graduate studies in math, physics, and chemistry

Notes: 22 January 2020

I was EXTREMELY impressed with the overall accessibility of the application. I definitely think it may be something students could use. Whoever built it, had a great knowledge of HTML accessibility.

There's a couple of things I see may present a problem for a student using this application. First, not all the list items are read out loud, or "tab-able", so that a blind person would know they are they. We can work around that by alerting them or putting in some sort of special link.

The second thing is that when the software "reads" the code, it doesn't read the backslashes. Oddly, and thankfully, it reads the backslashes as the student "writes" the code. There may very well be some adjustments to the screen reader I can make so it can "read" backslashes as well as announce them out loud when a student is writing; I'll be looking into that.
