Resources

On this page are examples of how museums have used alternative means to be more inclusive. MOMA has a permanent collection of "MakeyMakey" things; consider bringing in these devices to a classroom with blind students so that they may touch and feel the same devices that are on exhibit at MOMA. Or consider taking advantage of Museum Audio Guides at the Dali. Or sponsoring Street Art for the blnid. See the example of the street art for the blind jointly organized by the Mu.Cho Association for Inclusive Culture and the Museum of Visual Arts in Santiago.
